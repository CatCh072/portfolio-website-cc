Word Book

Let's have fun learning new words!

A vocabulary mobile app for children


  • Children can add words and word lists to their dictionary, study with flashcards and play games with their words.

  • Parents can monitor their child's dictionary and account.

  • Teachers can share their word lists with their students so they can study them on their own.

The Challenge

The challenge was to create a vocabulary learning application for our younger users. They need a way to study vocabulary words in an organized and engaging manner. Some kids found it boring to study from books and cumbersome to carry aound study materials.

User Research

User Research interviews were then conducted on 5 participants to understand how a child might learn new workds. These interviews provided insight to how to build a better learning experience for our users. I was surprised to learn that they didn't currently use an app for studying vocabulary.

Grade range: 2nd Grade to 8th Grade

The Goal

Word Book, a vocabulary learning application was created that lets our younger users have a personalized dictionary, flashcards and game options to help them study. This application also gives access to teachers and parents to monitor, add and edit their student's/child's words as needed.

What I have learned...

  • This project was completed as a part of CareerFoundry's Intro to UX Design course. Through this course, I have learned the basics of design processes,skills, and tools that is needed to become a UX Designer.
  • Through the process of building Word Book, I found how important my Persona was. I would add signifiers on my own accord, only to be reminded that this app was not for me but for my user. It was crucial to be focused on my user at all times and to always try to see things from her perspective.
  • Competitive Research and User Research, helped immensely to understand what was currently available for kids, what they use on a daily basis and what was missing for them.
